In this post we will see how to use three different tools to generate a phishing website and then send it to the victim using social engineering techniques.

A phishing attack is a type of cyber attack in which the attacker attempts to trick a victim into providing sensitive information, such as login credentials or financial information, by disguising themselves as a trustworthy entity. The process of a phishing attack typically involves the following steps:

  1. Creation of replica website: The attacker will create a replica website that is identical to the legitimate one (one website on which the victim has an account registered). They will often use similar logos, colors, and language to make the website look as authentic as possible. In our case we will use tools which will do that automatically for us. That means that our website choices are limited.

  2. Creating the message: The attacker will then create a message that appears to be from the legitimate source, such as an email or text message. The message will typically ask the recipient to click on a link or enter personal information on the replica website. In this tutorial we will use the kali's social engineering toolkit to generate an e-mail.

  3. Sending the message: The attacker will then send the message to a large number of individuals, (or even just one person "spear-phishing attack"). The message will appear to come from a legitimate source, such as a bank or company, and will often contain a sense of urgency (for example "ban of account") or a threat (for example "unknown login") to encourage the recipient to click on the link or enter personal information.

  4. Collecting sensitive information: Once the recipient clicks on the link or enters personal information on the replica website, the attacker will collect this information through Ngrok in our case and use it for their own gain, such as stealing money or committing identity theft.
